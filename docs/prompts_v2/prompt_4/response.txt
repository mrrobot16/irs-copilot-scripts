To file taxes based on the previous tax description, you would need to complete the following steps and forms:

1. Gather all necessary tax documents, including:

- Form W-2: Wage and Tax Statement from the startup where the client is employed.
- Form 1099-DIV: Dividends and Distributions, if applicable, for any dividends received from the trust's investment assets.
- Form 1099-B: Proceeds from Broker and Barter Exchange Transactions, if applicable, for any sales of stocks, bonds, or other investment assets.
- Form 1099-SA: Distributions from an HSA, if applicable, for any withdrawals from the HSA.
- Form 5498: IRA Contribution Information, if applicable, for the client's ROTH IRA contribution.

2. Determine the filing status: Single.

3. Calculate the client's taxable income and determine the appropriate tax deductions and credits.

4. Complete the necessary tax forms:

- Form 1040: U.S. Individual Income Tax Return.
- Schedule A: Itemized Deductions, if the client chooses to itemize deductions instead of taking the standard deduction.
- Schedule D: Capital Gains and Losses, to report any capital gains or losses from the trust's investment assets.
- Form 8889: Health Savings Accounts (HSA), if applicable, to report HSA contributions and withdrawals.
- Form 8606: Nondeductible IRAs, if applicable, to report the ROTH IRA contribution.

5. Attach any required supporting documentation, such as Form W-2, 1099s, and schedules, to the tax return.

6. Review the tax return for accuracy and ensure all necessary forms and schedules are included.

7. Sign and date the tax return.

8. File the tax return by the due date, which is generally April 15th, unless an extension is requested.
