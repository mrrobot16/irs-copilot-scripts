Schedule E (Form 1040) is a supplemental form for reporting supplemental income and loss from various sources such as rental real estate, royalties, partnerships, S corporations, estates, trusts, and REMICs. The form is attached to Form 1040, 1040-SR, 1040-NR, or 1041.

Part I of the form is used to report income or loss from rental real estate and royalties. It asks for information about any payments made in 2022 that would require the filing of Form 1099 and whether the required Form 1099 has been filed. It also asks for the physical address and type of each rental property, as well as the number of fair rental and personal use days for each property. The income and expenses related to the rental properties are reported in this section.

Part II is used to report income or loss from partnerships and S corporations. It asks for information about the partnerships and S corporations, including their names, employer identification numbers, and whether they are foreign partnerships. It also asks about passive and nonpassive income and losses from these entities.

Part III is used to report income or loss from estates and trusts. It asks for information about the estates and trusts, including their names and employer identification numbers. It also asks about passive and nonpassive income and losses from these entities.

Part IV is used to report income or loss from real estate mortgage investment conduits (REMICs) as a residual holder. It asks for information about the REMICs, including their names and employer identification numbers. It also asks about excess inclusions and taxable income from these entities.

Part V is a summary section that calculates the total income or loss from all sources reported on the form. It includes the net farm rental income or loss from Form 4835 and reconciliations for farming and fishing income and real estate professionals.

The form includes instructions and a website link for additional information. It is important to accurately report all income and expenses on Schedule E to comply with IRS regulations.
