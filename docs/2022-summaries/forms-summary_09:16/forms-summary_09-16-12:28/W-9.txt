Form W-9 is a document used by individuals or entities in the United States to provide their taxpayer identification number (TIN) to requesters who are required to file information returns with the IRS. The form includes sections for the individual or entity's name, business name (if applicable), federal tax classification, exemptions (if applicable), address, and TIN. The form also includes a certification section where the individual or entity certifies their TIN, exemption from backup withholding, status as a U.S. citizen or U.S. person, and correctness of any FATCA codes entered. The form provides instructions on how to complete each section and gives specific guidance for different types of accounts and entities. The form also includes information on backup withholding, FATCA reporting, updating information, penalties for failure to furnish a TIN, and protecting tax records from identity theft.
