Schedule V (Form 1120-F) is a document issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for corporations subject to a 4% tax rate under section 887. The form is used to provide information about vessels or aircraft owned or operated by the corporation.

The form consists of several lines that require the following information:

1. Name of the vessel or type of aircraft.
2. Vessel or aircraft ID number.
3. Whether the vessel or aircraft was chartered in.
4. Whether the vessel or aircraft was chartered out.
5. Name of the registered owner.
6. Employer identification number (EIN) or social security number (SSN) of the registered owner.
7. Name of the operator.
8. EIN or SSN of the operator.
9. U.S. source gross transportation income, with instructions to attach a statement describing the method used to determine this income.
10. Total U.S. source gross transportation income, calculated by adding the amounts from line 9.
11. Amount of income exempt by treaty, with instructions to attach Form 8833.
12. Result of subtracting line 11 from line 10, to be entered on Form 1120-F, Section I, line 9, column (b).

The form also includes a reference to the Instructions for Form 1120-F for further guidance and the IRS website for the latest information. Additionally, there is a mention of the Paperwork Reduction Act Notice.

Overall, Schedule V (Form 1120-F) is used to provide detailed information about vessels or aircraft owned or operated by a corporation subject to the 4% tax rate under section 887.
