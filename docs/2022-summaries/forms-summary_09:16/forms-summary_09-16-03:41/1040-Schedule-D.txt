Schedule D is a form used for reporting capital gains and losses on a taxpayer's federal tax return. It is attached to Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR. The form provides instructions and information on how to complete it. It is recommended to use Form 8949 to list transactions for certain lines on Schedule D. The form is divided into three parts: Part I for short-term capital gains and losses, Part II for long-term capital gains and losses, and Part III for summarizing the results. The form includes various lines for reporting different types of transactions, such as totals for transactions reported on Form 1099-B and Form 8949, and gains or losses from other forms such as Form 6252, Form 4684, and Schedule K-1. The form also includes instructions for calculating and reporting net gains or losses and provides worksheets for certain calculations.
