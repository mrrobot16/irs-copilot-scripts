Schedule S (Form 1120-F) is a form provided by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for corporations to claim the exclusion of income from the international operation of ships or aircraft under Section 883 of the tax code. The form is attached to Form 1120-F and requires the corporation to provide information about their qualified foreign corporation, the type of equivalent exemption granted by the foreign country, and the gross income in various categories of qualified income.

Part I of the form asks for the name of the qualified foreign country and the type of equivalent exemption granted. It also requires the corporation to provide the applicable authority for the exemption.

Part II asks for information related to the stock ownership test for publicly-traded corporations, including the country where the stock is primarily traded, the securities market on which the stock is listed, and a description of each class of stock relied on to meet the regularly traded test. It also asks if any 5% shareholders own 50% or more of the vote and value of the outstanding shares of any class of stock.

Part III is for the stock ownership test for controlled foreign corporations (CFC). It asks for the percentage of the value of all outstanding shares owned by qualified U.S. persons and the percentage owned as bearer shares maintained in a dematerialized or immobilized book-entry system. It also asks for the period during which the qualified U.S. persons held the stock and the period during which the foreign corporation was a CFC.

Part IV is for the qualified shareholder stock ownership test. It asks if more than 50% of the value of the outstanding shares of the corporation is owned by qualified shareholders for each category of income. It also asks for the total number of qualified shareholders, the percentage of the value of outstanding shares owned by qualified shareholders by country of residence or organization, the percentage owned as bearer shares maintained in a dematerialized or immobilized book-entry system, and the number of days during the tax year that the stock was held by qualified shareholders.

Overall, Schedule S (Form 1120-F) is a detailed form that requires corporations to provide information about their qualified foreign corporation and the ownership of stock in order to claim the exclusion of income from the international operation of ships or aircraft.
