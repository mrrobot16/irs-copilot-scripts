Form 1120-SF is the U.S. Income Tax Return for Settlement Funds under Section 468B. This form is used to report income and deductions for settlement funds for a specific calendar year. The form includes sections for reporting various types of income, such as taxable interest, dividends, capital gains, and income from partnership interests. Deductions can include trustee/administrator fees, taxes, accounting and legal services, and other expenses. The form also includes a tax computation section and a balance sheet section for reporting assets, liabilities, and fund balance. Additional information is required regarding transfers to the fund, tax-exempt interest received, distributions made to claimants, distributions made to transferors or related parties, the type of liability for which the fund was established, and the court order number under which the fund was established. The form must be signed by the fund administrator and may include information about a paid preparer.
