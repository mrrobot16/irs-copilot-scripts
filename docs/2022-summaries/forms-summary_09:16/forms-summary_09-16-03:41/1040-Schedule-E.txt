Schedule E (Form 1040) is a supplemental form used to report supplemental income and loss from various sources such as rental real estate, royalties, partnerships, S corporations, estates, trusts, and REMICs. 

The form is divided into several parts:

Part I: Income or Loss From Rental Real Estate and Royalties
- This section is used to report income or loss from rental real estate and royalties.
- It includes questions about making payments that require filing Form 1099 and whether Form 1099 has been filed.

Part II: Income or Loss From Partnerships and S Corporations
- This section is used to report income or loss from partnerships and S corporations.
- It includes information about reporting losses, distributions, stock disposal, and loan repayments.

Part III: Income or Loss From Estates and Trusts
- This section is used to report income or loss from estates and trusts.

Part IV: Income or Loss From Real Estate Mortgage Investment Conduits (REMICs) Residual Holder
- This section is used to report income or loss from REMICs.

Part V: Summary
- This section includes a summary of the income or loss reported in the previous sections.
- It includes the total income or loss from rental real estate, partnerships, S corporations, estates, trusts, and REMICs.
- It also includes sections for reconciling farming and fishing income and for reconciling real estate professional income.

The form includes various lines to report specific income and expenses related to rental real estate and royalties, as well as partnerships, S corporations, estates, trusts, and REMICs. It also provides instructions and references to additional forms and resources for further guidance.

Overall, Schedule E (Form 1040) is used to report supplemental income and loss from various sources and is attached to the main tax return form (Form 1040, 1040-SR, 1040-NR, or 1041).
