The document is a schedule (Schedule NEC) that is to be attached to Form 1040-NR for the tax year 2022. It is used to calculate the tax on income that is not effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business. The schedule provides different categories of income, such as dividends, interest, royalties, pensions, annuities, and capital gains. It also includes sections for reporting gambling winnings and losses. The taxpayer is required to enter the appropriate amounts under the corresponding tax rates provided. The total tax on income not effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business is calculated and entered on Form 1040-NR, line 23a. The schedule also includes a section for reporting capital gains and losses from property sales or exchanges that are not effectively connected with a U.S. business. Any gains or losses from disposing of a U.S. real property interest should be reported on Schedule D (Form 1040).
