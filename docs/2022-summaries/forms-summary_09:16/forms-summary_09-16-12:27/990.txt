Form 990 is a tax form that must be filed by organizations exempt from income tax under sections 501(c), 527, or 4947(a)(1) of the Internal Revenue Code. This form is used to report the organization's financial information, including revenue, expenses, assets, and liabilities. It also requires disclosure of compensation for officers, directors, trustees, key employees, and independent contractors. The form includes various schedules that provide additional details on specific aspects of the organization's activities and finances. The information reported on Form 990 is used by the IRS and the public to assess the organization's compliance with tax laws and its financial transparency.
