Form W-8EXP is a certificate issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for foreign governments, international organizations, foreign central banks of issue, foreign tax-exempt organizations, foreign private foundations, and governments of U.S. possessions. This form is used for United States tax withholding and reporting purposes.

The form is divided into four parts. Part I requires the identification of the beneficial owner, including the name of the organization, country of incorporation or organization, and type of entity. Part II is a qualification statement for Chapter 3 status, where the organization certifies its status as a foreign government, international organization, or foreign central bank of issue. Part III is a qualification statement for Chapter 4 status, which is required for certain reporting purposes. Part IV is a certification section where the authorized official of the organization declares that the information provided is true, correct, and complete.

The form also includes instructions on how to complete it and provides references to relevant sections of the Internal Revenue Code. It is important to note that this form is not to be sent to the IRS, but rather to the withholding agent or payer.
