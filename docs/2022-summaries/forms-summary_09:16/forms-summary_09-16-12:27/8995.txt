Form 8995 is a document issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) of the United States Department of the Treasury. It is used to calculate the Qualified Business Income Deduction, a tax deduction available to individuals with qualified business income from a qualified trade or business, real estate investment trust dividends, publicly traded partnership income, or a domestic production activities deduction passed through from an agricultural or horticultural cooperative.

The form is to be attached to the taxpayer's tax return and is applicable if the taxpayer's taxable income, before the qualified business income deduction, is at or below $170,050 ($340,100 if married filing jointly) and they are not a patron of an agricultural or horticultural cooperative.

The form consists of several sections and requires the taxpayer to provide information such as the trade, business, or aggregation name, taxpayer identification number, and the amount of qualified business income or loss. The form also includes calculations for determining the total qualified business income, qualified business net loss carryforward from the prior year, total qualified business income, qualified business income component, qualified REIT dividends and publicly traded partnership income or loss, qualified REIT dividends and qualified PTP loss carryforward from the prior year, total qualified REIT dividends and PTP income, REIT and PTP component, qualified business income deduction before the income limitation, taxable income before the qualified business income deduction, net capital gain, income limitation, and the final qualified business income deduction.

The form also includes sections for reporting total qualified business loss carryforward and total qualified REIT dividends and PTP loss carryforward, if applicable.

Taxpayers are advised to refer to the instructions provided on the IRS website (www.irs.gov/Form8995) for detailed guidance on completing the form.
