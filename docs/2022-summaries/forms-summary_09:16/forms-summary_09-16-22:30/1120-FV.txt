Schedule V (Form 1120-F) is a document provided by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) that should be attached to Form 1120-F. This form is used by corporations to provide information about vessels or aircrafts for which they are subject to a 4% tax rate under section 887. 

The form consists of several lines where the corporation must provide the following information for each vessel or aircraft:

1. Name of the vessel or type of aircraft.
2. Vessel or aircraft ID number.
3. Whether the vessel or aircraft was chartered in.
4. Whether the vessel or aircraft was chartered out.
5. Name of the registered owner.
6. Employer identification number (EIN) or social security number (SSN) of the registered owner.
7. Name of the operator.
8. EIN or SSN of the operator.
9. U.S. source gross transportation income, with instructions to attach a statement describing the method used to determine this income.
10. Total U.S. source gross transportation income, which is the sum of the amounts provided in line 9, columns A through D.
11. Amount of line 10 that is exempt by treaty, with instructions to attach Form 8833.
12. The result of subtracting line 11 from line 10, which should be entered on Form 1120-F, Section I, line 9, column (b).

The form also includes a reference to the Paperwork Reduction Act Notice.

For more detailed instructions and the most up-to-date information, taxpayers are directed to visit the IRS website at www.irs.gov/Form1120F.
