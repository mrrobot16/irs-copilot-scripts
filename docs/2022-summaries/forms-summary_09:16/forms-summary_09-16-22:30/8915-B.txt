Form 8915-B is a document issued by the Department of the Treasury and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) in the United States. It is used to report qualified 2017 disaster retirement plan distributions and repayments. The form is to be attached to the taxpayer's 2021 Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR.

The form consists of two parts. Part I is for reporting qualified 2017 disaster distributions from retirement plans other than IRAs. It includes fields to enter the amounts from the taxpayer's 2020 Form 8915-B, lines 1a, 1b, 2a, and 2b, as well as any repayments made in 2021 that were not included on the 2020 form. The form also calculates the excess repayment and the total amount that can be carried back to reduce the amount subject to tax in a prior year.

Part II of the form is for reporting qualified 2017 disaster distributions from traditional, SEP, SIMPLE, and Roth IRAs. It includes fields to enter the amounts from the taxpayer's 2020 Form 8915-B, lines 6a, 6b, 7a, and 7b, as well as any repayments made in 2021 that were not included on the 2020 form. The form also calculates the excess repayment and the total amount that can be carried back to reduce the amount subject to tax in a prior year.

The form requires the taxpayer's name, social security number, and address. It also includes a declaration section to be signed by the taxpayer under penalties of perjury.

For more detailed instructions and the latest information, taxpayers are directed to visit the IRS website at www.irs.gov/Form8915B.
