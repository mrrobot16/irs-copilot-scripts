Schedule 2 (Form 1040) is an additional tax form that taxpayers need to attach to their Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR. It includes two parts: Part I for Tax and Part II for Other Taxes.

In Part I, taxpayers need to provide information about the alternative minimum tax and excess advance premium tax credit repayment. They need to attach Form 6251 for alternative minimum tax and Form 8962 for excess advance premium tax credit repayment. The total of lines 1 and 2 should be entered on Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR, line 17.

In Part II, taxpayers need to provide information about various other taxes. This includes self-employment tax, social security and Medicare tax on unreported tip income, uncollected social security and Medicare tax on wages, total additional social security and Medicare tax, additional tax on IRAs or other tax-favored accounts (attach Form 5329 if required), household employment taxes (attach Schedule H), repayment of first-time homebuyer credit (attach Form 5405 if required), additional Medicare Tax (attach Form 8959), net investment income tax (attach Form 8960), uncollected social security and Medicare or RRTA tax on tips or group-term life insurance from Form W-2, box 12, interest on tax due on installment income from the sale of certain residential lots and timeshares, interest on the deferred tax on gain from certain installment sales with a sales price over $150,000, and recapture of low-income housing credit (attach Form 8611).

Part II also includes a section for other additional taxes, such as recapture of other credits, recapture of federal mortgage subsidy, additional tax on HSA distributions, additional tax on an HSA because the taxpayer didn't remain an eligible individual, additional tax on Archer MSA distributions, additional tax on Medicare Advantage MSA distributions, recapture of a charitable contribution deduction related to a fractional interest in tangible personal property, income received from a nonqualified deferred compensation plan that fails to meet the requirements of section 409A, compensation received from a nonqualified deferred compensation plan described in section 457A, Section 72(m)(5) excess benefits tax, golden parachute payments, tax on accumulation distribution of trusts, excise tax on insider stock compensation from an expatriated corporation, look-back interest under section 167(g) or 460(b) from Form 8697 or 8866, tax on non-effectively connected income for any part of the year the taxpayer was a nonresident alien from Form 1040-NR, interest from Form 8621, line 16f relating to distributions from, and dispositions of, stock of a section 1291 fund, interest from Form 8621, line 24, and any other taxes.

The total of lines 17a through 17z is calculated on line 18, and line 21 is the sum of lines 4, 7 through 16, and 18. This total should be entered on Form 1040 or 1040-SR, line 23, or Form 1040-NR, line 23b.

Please note that this summary is based on the 2022 version of Schedule 2 (Form 1040) and may be subject to change in future years. Taxpayers should refer to the official IRS instructions for the most up-to-date information.
