Schedule OI (Form 1040-NR) is a supplementary form for individuals filing Form 1040-NR, which is used for reporting U.S. income tax returns by nonresident aliens. The purpose of Schedule OI is to gather additional information from the taxpayer regarding their citizenship, residency, immigration status, and other relevant details.

The form includes several sections and questions that the taxpayer must answer. These include:

A. Citizenship: The taxpayer is required to indicate the country or countries they were a citizen or national of during the tax year.

B. Residence: The taxpayer must specify the country they claimed residence for tax purposes during the tax year.

C. Green Card Application: The taxpayer is asked whether they have ever applied to be a green card holder (lawful permanent resident) of the United States.

D. U.S. Citizenship and Green Card Holder Status: The taxpayer must indicate whether they have ever been a U.S. citizen or a green card holder.

E. Visa or Immigration Status: The taxpayer is required to provide their visa type or U.S. immigration status on the last day of the tax year.

F. Visa or Immigration Status Change: The taxpayer must indicate whether they have ever changed their visa type or U.S. immigration status and provide the date and nature of the change if applicable.

G. Entry and Departure Dates: The taxpayer is asked to list all dates they entered and left the United States during the tax year.

H. Days Present in the United States: The taxpayer must provide the number of days they were present in the United States during the tax years 2020, 2021, and 2022.

I. Prior Year Tax Returns: The taxpayer is asked whether they have filed a U.S. income tax return for any prior year and to provide the latest year and form number they filed.

J. Trust Filing: The taxpayer must indicate whether they are filing a return for a trust and whether the trust had a U.S. or foreign owner, made a distribution or loan to a U.S. person, or received a contribution from a U.S. person.

K. Compensation Amount: The taxpayer is asked whether they received total compensation of $250,000 or more during the tax year and whether they used an alternative method to determine the source of this compensation.

L. Income Exempt From Tax: If the taxpayer is claiming exemption from income tax under a U.S. income tax treaty with a foreign country, they must provide the necessary details.

M. Election for Real Property Income: The taxpayer is asked to indicate whether they are making an election or have previously made an election to treat income from real property located in the United States as effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business.

The form also includes instructions and references to additional resources for further guidance.
