Schedule V (Form 1120-F) is a document that must be attached to Form 1120-F, which is used by corporations to report their income tax return for foreign corporations engaged in a trade or business in the United States. The purpose of Schedule V is to provide information about vessels or aircraft owned or operated by the corporation.

The form requests the following information for each vessel or aircraft:

1. Name of the vessel or type of aircraft.
2. Vessel or aircraft ID number.
3. Whether the vessel or aircraft was chartered in.
4. Whether the vessel or aircraft was chartered out.
5. Name of the registered owner.
6. Employer identification number (EIN) or social security number (SSN) of the registered owner.
7. Name of the operator.
8. EIN or SSN of the operator.
9. U.S. source gross transportation income, with instructions to attach a statement describing the method used to determine this income.
10. Total U.S. source gross transportation income, calculated by adding the amounts from line 9.
11. Amount of line 10 that is exempt by treaty, with instructions to attach Form 8833.
12. The result of subtracting line 11 from line 10, which should be entered on Form 1120-F, Section I, line 9, column (b).

The form includes a notice regarding the Paperwork Reduction Act and provides a website where taxpayers can find instructions and the latest information about the form.

Overall, Schedule V is a necessary attachment to Form 1120-F for corporations subject to a 4% tax rate under section 887, and it provides details about vessels or aircraft owned or operated by the corporation and their associated income.
