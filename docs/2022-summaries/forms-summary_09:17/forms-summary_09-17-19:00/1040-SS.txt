Form 1040-SS is a U.S. Self-Employment Tax Return form for individuals who are self-employed and residents of the U.S. Virgin Islands, Guam, American Samoa, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, or Puerto Rico. The form is used to report self-employment income, calculate self-employment tax, and claim certain tax credits.

The form requires the individual to provide their personal information, including their name, social security number, and address. It also asks about any digital asset transactions and the filing status of the individual.

The form has several parts, including Total Tax and Credits, Bona Fide Residents of Puerto Rico Claiming Additional Child Tax Credit, Profit or Loss from Farming, Profit or Loss from Business (Sole Proprietorship), and Self-Employment Tax. Each part requires the individual to report their income, expenses, and other relevant information.

The form also includes instructions and explanations for each section, as well as spaces for the individual to sign and provide contact information. There are also sections for a third-party designee and a paid preparer, if applicable.

Overall, Form 1040-SS is a comprehensive tax return form specifically designed for self-employed individuals in certain U.S. territories to report their income and calculate their tax liability.
