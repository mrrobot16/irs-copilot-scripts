Form 5472 is an information return that must be filed by certain foreign-owned U.S. corporations or foreign corporations engaged in a U.S. trade or business. The form is used to report information about the reporting corporation, its 25% foreign shareholders, related parties, and transactions with foreign related parties. 

Part I of the form requires the reporting corporation to provide its name, address, employer identification number, total assets, principal business activity, and other relevant information. 

Part II requires the reporting corporation to provide information about its 25% foreign shareholders, including their names, addresses, identification numbers, and countries where business is conducted. 

Part III requires the reporting corporation to provide information about related parties, including their names, addresses, identification numbers, principal business activity, and relationship to the reporting corporation or its foreign shareholders. 

Part IV requires the reporting corporation to report monetary transactions between itself and foreign related parties, including sales of stock, tangible property, platform contribution transaction payments, cost sharing transaction payments, rents, royalties, and other amounts received or paid. 

Part V allows the reporting corporation to report any other reportable transactions, such as contributions to or distributions from the entity. 

Part VI allows the reporting corporation to report nonmonetary and less-than-full consideration transactions with foreign related parties. 

Part VII requires the reporting corporation to provide additional information, including whether it imports goods from a foreign related party, whether it participated in a cost sharing arrangement, and whether it claimed certain deductions or had certain loan transactions with related parties. 

Part VIII is for reporting information about cost sharing arrangements in which the reporting corporation participated. 

Part IX is for reporting base erosion payments and base erosion tax benefits under section 59A of the Internal Revenue Code. 

The form must be completed in English and all monetary amounts must be reported in U.S. dollars. The form includes instructions for completing each part and additional information can be found on the IRS website.
