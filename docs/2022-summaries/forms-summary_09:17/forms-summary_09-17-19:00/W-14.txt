Form W-14 is a certificate that is used by foreign contracting parties who receive federal procurement payments in the United States. The form requires the identification of the foreign contracting party and the acquiring agency, including their names, addresses, and taxpayer identification numbers. 

The form also includes sections for claiming exemptions based on international agreements or procurement agreements, as well as providing explanations for the basis of these exemptions. 

The certificate must be signed by the foreign person or their authorized representative, certifying that the information provided is true and complete. The form also includes a statement acknowledging the obligation to pay any applicable taxes and penalties.
