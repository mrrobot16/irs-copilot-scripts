Form 8881 is a document issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for taxpayers to claim the Credit for Small Employer Pension Plan Startup Costs and Auto-Enrollment. The form is to be attached to the taxpayer's tax return.

Part I of the form is for claiming the credit for small employer pension plan startup costs. The taxpayer is required to enter the qualified startup costs incurred during the tax year on line 1. On line 2, they should enter one-half of the amount from line 1. The number of employees eligible to participate in the pension plan should be entered on line 3. On line 4, the taxpayer should enter the greater of $500 or the amount from line 3, but not more than $5,000. Line 5 requires the taxpayer to enter the smaller of the amounts from line 2 or line 4. Line 6 is reserved for partnerships and S corporations. The taxpayer should add lines 5 and 6 and report the total amount on line 8. Partnerships and S corporations should report this amount on Schedule K, while others should report it on Form 3800, Part III, line 1j.

Part II of the form is for claiming the small employer auto-enrollment credit. The taxpayer should enter $500 on line 9 if they provide an auto-enrollment option for retirement savings. Line 10 is reserved for partnerships and S corporations. The taxpayer should add lines 9 and 10 and report the total amount on line 11. Partnerships and S corporations should report this amount on Schedule K, while others should report it on Form 3800, Part III, line 1j.
