Form 945 is an annual return used to report withheld federal income tax for withholding reported on Forms 1099 and W-2G. The form includes sections for providing information such as the employer's identification number (EIN), address, and trade name. It also includes sections for reporting federal income tax withheld from various sources, backup withholding, total taxes, total deposits, balance due, and overpayment. The form also includes a section for monthly summary of federal tax liability. The form can be used by both monthly schedule depositors and semiweekly schedule depositors. The form also includes a section for designating a third-party to discuss the return with the IRS. The form must be signed and dated by the taxpayer or preparer. The form also includes a payment voucher, Form 945-V, which can be used to make a payment with Form 945. The voucher includes sections for providing the employer identification number, amount of payment, business name, address, and payment details. The voucher should be detached and mailed with the payment and Form 945.
