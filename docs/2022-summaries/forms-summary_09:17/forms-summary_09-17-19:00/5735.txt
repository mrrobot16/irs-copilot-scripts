Form 5735 is a document issued by the Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for corporations to claim the American Samoa Economic Development Credit. This form should be attached to the corporation's tax return.

The form requires the corporation to meet the qualified production activities income requirement in order to qualify for the credit. The corporation must enter various percentages of qualified compensation and depreciation deductions for short-life, medium-life, and long-life qualified tangible property.

The form calculates the tentative credit by adding the amounts entered on lines 1 through 4. The total U.S. income tax against which the credit is allowed is entered on line 6. The American Samoa economic development credit is then determined by entering the smaller of line 5 or line 6 on line 7. This amount should also be reported on Form 1120, Schedule J, line 5b, or the corresponding line on other tax returns.

The form includes a caution that the corporation must meet the qualified production activities income requirement to qualify for the credit. It also provides an OMB number and a space for the corporation's name and employer identification number.

The document includes a notice regarding the Paperwork Reduction Act, and the revision date of the form is January 2013.
