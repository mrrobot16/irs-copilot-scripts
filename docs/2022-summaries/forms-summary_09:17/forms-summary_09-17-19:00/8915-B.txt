Form 8915-B is a document issued by the Department of the Treasury and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) in the United States. It is used for reporting qualified 2017 disaster retirement plan distributions and repayments. The form is to be attached to the taxpayer's 2021 Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR.

The form consists of two parts: Part I for reporting qualified 2017 disaster distributions from retirement plans other than IRAs, and Part II for reporting qualified 2017 disaster distributions from traditional, SEP, SIMPLE, and Roth IRAs.

The form requires the taxpayer to provide personal information, including their name, social security number, and address. It also includes various lines for reporting the amounts from the taxpayer's 2020 Form 8915-B, such as excess repayments from 2020 and repayments made in 2021.

The form provides instructions for completing each line and includes a signature section for the taxpayer to declare the accuracy of the information provided.

For more detailed instructions and the latest information, taxpayers are directed to visit the IRS website at www.irs.gov/Form8915B. The form also mentions that for coronavirus-related distributions, taxpayers should refer to Form 8915-F.

It is important to note that this summary is a general overview and taxpayers should consult the official instructions and seek professional advice to ensure accurate completion of the form.
