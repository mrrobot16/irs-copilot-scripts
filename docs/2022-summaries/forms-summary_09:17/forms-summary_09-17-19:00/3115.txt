Form 3115 is an application for a change in accounting method for tax purposes. The form is used by businesses or individuals to request a change in the way they account for income, expenses, depreciation, or amortization. The form requires the filer to provide information such as their identification number, principal business activity, and contact information. The form also asks for details about the specific change being requested, including the present and proposed accounting methods. The filer must provide a detailed explanation and supporting documentation for the change. The form includes schedules for changes related to overall method of accounting, deferral method for advance payments, cost offset methods, LIFO inventory method, long-term contracts, and depreciation or amortization. The form must be signed under penalties of perjury, and the filer must declare that the information provided is true, correct, and complete.
