Form W-9 is a document used by individuals and entities in the United States to provide their taxpayer identification number (TIN) to requesters who are required to file information returns with the IRS. The form includes sections for the individual or entity's name, business name (if applicable), federal tax classification, exemptions, address, and TIN. The form also includes a certification section where the individual or entity certifies their TIN, exemption from backup withholding, U.S. citizenship or status as a U.S. person, and exemption from FATCA reporting. The form provides specific instructions for completing each section and includes information on how to obtain a TIN and protect against identity theft.
