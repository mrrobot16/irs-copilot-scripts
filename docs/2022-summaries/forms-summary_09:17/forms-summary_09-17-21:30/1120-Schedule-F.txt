Form 1120-SF is an income tax return form for settlement funds under Section 468B of the Internal Revenue Code. The form is used to report income, deductions, and tax computation for settlement funds. 

Part I of the form is used to report various types of income, including taxable interest, dividends, capital gains, income or gain from a partnership interest, and other income. Deductions such as trustee/administrator fees, taxes, accounting and legal services, and other expenses are also reported in Part I.

Part II is used to compute the tax liability based on the modified gross income. The form provides space to enter credits and payments, including overpayments from prior years, current year estimated tax payments, and tax deposits made with Form 7004. The estimated tax penalty can also be calculated and reported on this form.

Schedule L is used to report the balance sheet of the settlement fund, including assets, liabilities, and fund balance at the beginning and end of the year. Additional information is also required, such as the amount of cash and property transferred to the fund during the tax year, tax-exempt interest received or accrued, distributions made to claimants, distributions made to transferors or related parties, and the type of liability for which the fund was established.

The form requires the signature of the fund administrator and includes a section for the declaration of the preparer, if applicable. The form also includes sections for additional information and a space to enter the Court Order Number if the fund was established by a court order.

Overall, Form 1120-SF is used to report the income, deductions, and tax liability for settlement funds and provides a comprehensive overview of the financial position of the fund.
