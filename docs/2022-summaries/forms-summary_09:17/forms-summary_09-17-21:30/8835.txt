Form 8835 is a document issued by the Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service (IRS) in the United States. It is used to claim the Renewable Electricity Production Credit on a tax return. The form consists of several lines where the taxpayer must enter the amount of electricity produced and sold at qualified facilities using different renewable energy sources such as wind, closed-loop biomass, geothermal, solar, open-loop biomass, landfill gas, trash, hydropower, and marine and hydrokinetic renewables. Each source has a specific rate associated with it. The form also includes lines for calculating the total credit, phaseout adjustment, reduction for government grants, and other adjustments. The final result is the renewable electricity production credit that can be claimed on the tax return. The form also provides instructions and a website link for further information.
