Form 1042-S is a document issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for reporting foreign persons' U.S. source income subject to withholding. The form contains various boxes and codes that provide information about the income, tax withheld, withholding agent, recipient, and other related details. It is used to report different types of income, such as interest, dividends, royalties, pensions, and more. The form also includes exemption codes, tax rates, withholding allowances, and information about the withholding agent's EIN, name, and address, as well as the recipient's name, country code, address, and TIN. The form is available in different copies (A, B, C, D, E) for different purposes, such as filing with the IRS, attaching to a federal tax return, attaching to a state tax return, and providing to the recipient.
