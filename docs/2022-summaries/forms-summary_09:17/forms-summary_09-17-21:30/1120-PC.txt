Form 1120-PC is the income tax return form for U.S. Property and Casualty Insurance Companies for the calendar year 2022 or tax year beginning in 2022. The form is used to report taxable income, tax computation and payments, and other relevant information for the insurance company. The form includes various schedules such as Schedule A for taxable income, Schedule B for taxable investment income, Schedule C for dividends, inclusions, and other special deductions, Schedule E for premiums earned, Schedule F for losses incurred, Schedule G for other capital losses, Schedule H for special deduction and ending adjusted surplus for section 833 organizations, Schedule I for other information, Schedule L for balance sheets per books, Schedule M-1 for reconciliation of income per books with income per return, and Schedule M-2 for analysis of unappropriated retained earnings per books. The form also includes sections for providing information on the accounting method, ownership of voting stock, affiliated group status, foreign ownership, NOL carryover, Blue Cross or Blue Shield organization status, small company election, gross receipts, interest and royalty disallowance, section 163(j) election, and Form 8990 filing requirement.
