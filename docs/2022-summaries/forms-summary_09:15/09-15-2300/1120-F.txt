Form 1120-F is the U.S. Income Tax Return of a Foreign Corporation. It is used by foreign corporations to report their income and expenses for tax purposes in the United States. The form includes sections for providing information about the corporation, such as its name, address, and country of incorporation. It also includes sections for reporting income from U.S. sources that is not effectively connected with the conduct of a trade or business in the United States, as well as income that is effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business. The form also includes schedules for calculating the branch profits tax and the tax on excess interest. Additionally, the form includes a balance sheet section for reporting the corporation's assets, liabilities, and equity.
