Form 1120-S is the U.S. Income Tax Return for an S Corporation. This form should only be filed if the corporation has filed or is attaching Form 2553 to elect to be an S corporation. The form includes various sections for providing information such as the corporation's name, address, employer identification number, business activity code, and total assets. It also includes sections for reporting income, deductions, tax and payments, and shareholder pro rata share items. Additionally, there are schedules for providing other information, balance sheets per books, and reconciling income per books with income per return. The form is used to accurately report the corporation's income and determine its tax liability.
