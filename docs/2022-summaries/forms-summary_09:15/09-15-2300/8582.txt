Form 8582 is a document provided by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) that is used to calculate and report passive activity loss limitations. It is attached to Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1041. The form consists of several parts, including Part I, which deals with rental real estate activities with active participation, and Part II, which covers special allowances for rental real estate activities with active participation. There are also parts for total losses allowed, allocation of unallowed losses, allowed losses, and activities with losses reported on two or more forms or schedules. The form includes various lines and columns where taxpayers enter information related to their passive activity income, losses, and prior year unallowed losses. The form provides instructions for completing each part and includes references to additional resources on the IRS website.
