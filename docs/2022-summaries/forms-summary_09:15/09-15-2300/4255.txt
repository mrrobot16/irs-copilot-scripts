Form 4255 is a document from the Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service, used for the recapture of investment credit. It should be attached to the taxpayer's income tax return. The form provides instructions and information about recapturing investment credits for various types of properties, including rehabilitation, energy, qualifying advanced coal project, qualifying gasification project, qualifying advanced energy project, and qualifying therapeutic discovery project property. The form is divided into three parts: Part I deals with the original investment credit, Part II addresses recapture from an increase in nonqualified nonrecourse financing, and Part III covers recapture from the disposition of property or cessation of use as investment credit property. The form includes various lines for entering relevant information, such as the original rate of credit, credit base, net change in nonqualified nonrecourse financing, refigured credit, credit taken in prior years, recapture due to a net increase in nonqualified nonrecourse financing, unused general business credits, recapture tax as a result of a net increase in nonqualified nonrecourse financing, date the property was placed in service, date the property ceased to be qualified investment credit property, number of full years between those dates, unused general business credits without credit from the property, recapture percentage, multiplication of line 14 by the percentage on line 15, and the total increase in tax. The form also includes a section for recapture of qualifying therapeutic discovery project grants. The taxpayer should enter the total increase in tax on the appropriate line of their tax return.
