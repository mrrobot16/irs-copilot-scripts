Form 8288 is a U.S. Withholding Tax Return for Certain Dispositions by Foreign Persons. It is used by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to collect withholding tax from foreign persons who dispose of U.S. real property interests or partnership interests. The form includes sections for the withholding agent information, the buyer or transferee required to withhold under Section 1445(a), an entity subject to the provisions of Section 1445(e), a buyer or transferee required to withhold under Section 1446(f)(1), and a buyer or transferee claiming a refund of withholding under Section 1446(f)(4). Each section requires the completion of specific information related to the transaction and the calculation of withholding tax liability. The form must be signed by the withholding agent, partner, fiduciary, or corporate officer under penalties of perjury.
