Form 3800 is a document from the Department of the Treasury and Internal Revenue Service that pertains to the General Business Credit. It provides instructions and information for completing the form. The form must be attached to the tax return and includes three parts.

Part I is for the Current Year Credit for Credits Not Allowed Against Tentative Minimum Tax (TMT). It includes various lines for calculating the general business credit and passive activity credits.

Part II is for the Allowable Credit and involves calculations related to regular tax before credits and alternative minimum tax.

Part III is for General Business Credits or Eligible Small Business Credits. This part includes separate sections for different types of credits, such as investment, advanced manufacturing production, increasing research activities, low-income housing, and more.

Overall, Form 3800 is a detailed form that taxpayers must complete to claim the General Business Credit on their tax return.
