Form W-14 is a certificate that is used by a foreign contracting party to provide information to the acquiring agency when receiving federal procurement payments. The form includes sections for identifying the foreign contracting party and the acquiring agency, as well as options for claiming exemptions based on international agreements or procurement agreements. The form also includes a section for providing explanations and a certificate to be signed under penalties of perjury. The form should be given to the acquiring agency and should not be sent to the IRS.
