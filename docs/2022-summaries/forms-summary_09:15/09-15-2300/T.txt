Form T (Timber) is a document provided by the Internal Revenue Service for reporting forest activities and timber depletion for tax purposes. The form includes several parts, including acquisitions, timber depletion, profit or loss from land and timber sales, reforestation and timber stand activities, and land ownership. The form requires information such as the name of the property, location, date of acquisition, amount paid, legal expenses, and total cost or other basis of the property. It also requires information on timber quantity, estimated quantity returnable through depletion, additions for growth, timber acquired during the tax year, and depletion for the current tax year. The form also includes sections for reporting timber sales and other consideration received, as well as reforestation expenses and changes in land ownership.
