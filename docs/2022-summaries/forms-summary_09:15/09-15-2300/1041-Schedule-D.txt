Schedule D (Form 1041) is a document used for reporting capital gains and losses for estates and trusts. It should be attached to Form 1041, Form 5227, or Form 990-T. The form provides instructions on how to calculate and report the amounts for short-term and long-term capital gains and losses. It also includes sections for reporting gains or losses from specific forms, such as Forms 4684, 6252, 6781, 8824, and 4797. The form also includes sections for reporting capital gain distributions and capital loss carryovers. Part III of the form summarizes the information from Parts I and II, and Part IV provides instructions for the capital loss limitation. Part V is used for computing taxes using maximum capital gains rates. The form includes various lines and columns for entering the relevant information, and taxpayers should refer to the instructions for detailed guidance on completing the form.
