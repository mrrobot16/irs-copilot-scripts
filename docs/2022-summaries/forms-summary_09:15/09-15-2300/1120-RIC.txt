Form 1120-RIC is the U.S. Income Tax Return for Regulated Investment Companies. The form is used to report the taxable income of regulated investment companies for the tax year. The form requires information such as the name and address of the fund, the year of RIC status election, the date the fund was established, and the employer identification number. It also includes sections for reporting investment company taxable income, deductions, and tax and payments. The form also includes schedules for reporting information related to dividends paid, tax-exempt obligations, and balance sheets.
