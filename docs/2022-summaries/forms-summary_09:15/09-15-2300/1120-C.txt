Form 1120 is the U.S. Corporation Income Tax Return for calendar year 2022 or tax year beginning in 2022 and ending in 20. The form is used by corporations to report their income and calculate their tax liability. The form requires information such as the corporation's name, address, employer identification number, date of incorporation, total assets, gross receipts or sales, cost of goods sold, dividends and inclusions, interest, rents, royalties, capital gains, and other income. It also includes deductions for compensation of officers, salaries and wages, repairs and maintenance, bad debts, rents, taxes and licenses, interest, charitable contributions, depreciation, depletion, advertising, pension and profit-sharing plans, employee benefit programs, and other deductions. The form also includes schedules for dividends, inclusions, and special deductions, tax computation and payment, and other information such as accounting method, business activity, ownership changes, and balance sheets. The form requires the signature of an officer and may require the attachment of additional schedules and statements.
