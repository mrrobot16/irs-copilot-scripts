Form 1139 is a document used by corporations to apply for a tentative refund of taxes. The form requires the corporation to provide information such as their name, employer identification number, and address. The corporation must also indicate the reason for filing the form, such as a net operating loss or unused general business credit. The form includes sections for the corporation to provide details about the tax year of loss or overpayment, any foreign tax credits, and whether a consolidated return was filed. The corporation must also indicate if they have filed a petition in Tax Court for the relevant year. The form includes a computation section for calculating the decrease in tax, which includes factors such as taxable income, capital loss carryback, and various tax credits. The form must be signed by an officer of the corporation and includes a section for a paid preparer if applicable.
