Form 8918 is a Material Advisor Disclosure Statement form issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for reporting certain transactions. The form is used by material advisors to disclose information about reportable transactions and provide details about their involvement in these transactions. 

The form requires the material advisor to provide their name, contact information, and identifying number. It also asks for information about the reportable transaction, including its name, type, and any published guidance related to it. The material advisor must indicate if they are a party to a designation agreement and provide information about the other parties involved.

The form also asks the material advisor to provide a brief description of the type of material aid, assistance, or advice they provided in the transaction, as well as any other entities or individuals involved in providing such aid or advice.

The form asks whether related entities or individuals, foreign entities or individuals, or tax-exempt entities are needed to obtain the intended tax benefits of the transaction. If so, the material advisor must describe the role of each individual or entity and provide their country of existence if required.

The form also asks about the types of financial instruments used in the transaction and the estimated tax benefits expected to be claimed by the taxpayer. The material advisor must identify the type of tax benefit generated by the transaction and indicate the timing of when these tax benefits are claimed.

The form requires the material advisor to provide the Internal Revenue Code sections used to claim the tax benefits generated by the transaction. There is also a section to describe the reportable transaction in detail, including the expected tax treatment and benefits, the role of entities or individuals involved, and the application of the Internal Revenue Code sections.

The form must be signed by the material advisor under penalties of perjury, declaring that the information provided is true, correct, and complete.
