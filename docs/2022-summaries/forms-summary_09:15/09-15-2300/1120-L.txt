Form 1120-L is the U.S. Life Insurance Company Income Tax Return for calendar year 2022 or the tax year beginning in 2022. The form is used by life insurance companies to report their income, deductions, and tax liability. The form includes various sections for reporting gross premiums, net decrease in reserves, investment income, capital gain net income, and other income. It also includes sections for reporting deductions such as death benefits, net increase in reserves, deductible policyholder dividends, and interest expenses. The form also requires information on the corporation's assets, insurance liabilities, and other information such as accounting method, ownership structure, and tax elections.
