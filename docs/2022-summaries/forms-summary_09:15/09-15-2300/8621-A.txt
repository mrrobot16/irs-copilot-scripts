Form 8621-A is a document provided by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) for shareholders who wish to make certain late elections to end treatment as a Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC). The form includes instructions and sections for shareholders to provide their identifying information, the type of shareholder filing the return, and contact information. It also includes different election options for shareholders, including late deemed dividend elections and late deemed sale elections with respect to former PFICs or Section 1297(e) PFICs. The form also includes sections for shareholders to provide information about the termination date or CFC qualification date, the taxable year, and the pro rata share of post-1986 earnings and profits. Additionally, there is a section for shareholders to compute the tax and interest due, including information about excess distributions, income tax liability, and foreign tax credits. The form concludes with a closing agreement and a balance sheet for the election year.
