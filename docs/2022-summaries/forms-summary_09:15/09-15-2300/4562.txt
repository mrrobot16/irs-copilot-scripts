Form 4562 is a document provided by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) that is used for reporting depreciation and amortization expenses for tax purposes. The form is attached to the tax return and includes information on listed property.

The form is divided into several parts. Part I is used to elect to expense certain property under Section 179 of the tax code. It includes information on the maximum amount that can be expensed, the total cost of Section 179 property placed in service, and the reduction in limitation.

Part II is used to report special depreciation allowances and other depreciation expenses for qualified property placed in service during the tax year.

Part III is used to report MACRS (Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System) depreciation for assets placed in service in tax years before 2022.

Part IV provides a summary of the information reported in Parts I, II, and III.

Part V is specifically for listed property, such as automobiles, certain vehicles, aircraft, and property used for entertainment, recreation, or amusement. It includes information on depreciation and other details related to listed property.

Part VI is used for reporting amortization expenses for costs that begin during the tax year or that began before the tax year.

The form includes various sections and columns where specific information needs to be entered, such as the type of property, date placed in service, business/investment use percentage, cost or other basis, recovery period, method/convention, and depreciation deduction.

It's important to refer to the instructions provided by the IRS for detailed guidance on how to complete Form 4562 accurately. The form is for the tax year 2022.
