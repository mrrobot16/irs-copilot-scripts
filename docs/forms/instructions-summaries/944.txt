<h3>Summary of 2022 Instructions for Form 944</h3>
<p>The IRS Form 944 is designed for the smallest employers (those whose annual liability for social security, Medicare, and withheld federal income taxes is $1,000 or less) to file and pay these taxes once a year instead of every quarter. The form is used to report wages paid, tips employees reported, federal income tax withheld, and both the employer and employee shares of social security and Medicare taxes.</p>

<h3>Key Sections of the Form</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Employer identification number (EIN), name, and address.</li>
  <li>Wages, tips, and other compensation.</li>
  <li>Federal income tax withheld.</li>
  <li>Social security and Medicare taxes.</li>
  <li>Adjustments for current year's corrections.</li>
  <li>Total taxes after adjustments and nonrefundable credits.</li>
  <li>Deposits made and balance due or overpayment.</li>
  <li>Third-party designee information.</li>
  <li>Signature of the business owner or authorized representative.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Filing and Payment Information</h3>
<p>Employers must file Form 944 annually by January 31st of the following year. If all taxes were deposited in full and on time, the deadline extends to February 10th. Taxes can be paid with the return if the total taxes for the year are less than $2,500. Otherwise, taxes should be deposited according to the deposit schedule.</p>

<h3>Important Notes</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Employers can allow a third-party to discuss their Form 944 with the IRS by completing the third-party designee section.</li>
  <li>A paid preparer must sign the form if they were compensated for preparing it.</li>
  <li>The form includes worksheets to help calculate certain tax credits related to sick and family leave wages and COBRA premium assistance payments.</li>
</ul>
