<h2>Summary of Instructions for Form 8038</h2>
<p><b>Form 8038</b> is used by issuers of tax-exempt private activity bonds to provide information to the IRS as required by section 149 and to monitor compliance with sections 141 through 150.</p>

<h3>Who Must File</h3>
<p>Issuers of tax-exempt private activity bonds issued after 1986, including various types like exempt facility bonds, qualified mortgage bonds, and qualified 501(c)(3) bonds, must file Form 8038 for each issue.</p>

<h3>When To File</h3>
<p>The form should be filed by the 15th day of the 2nd calendar month after the calendar quarter end in which the bond was issued. Late filings may be granted an extension under certain circumstances.</p>

<h3>Where To File</h3>
<p>File Form 8038 and any attachments at the specified address for the Department of the Treasury, IRS Center in Ogden, UT.</p>

<h3>Other Forms That May Be Required</h3>
<p>Other forms like Form 8038-G, Form 8038-GC, or Form 8038-T may be required for other types of tax-exempt bonds or for arbitrage rebate purposes.</p>

<h3>Definitions</h3>
<p>Various terms like tax-exempt bond, taxable bond, private activity bond, and exempt facility bond are defined for clarity.</p>

<h3>Specific Instructions</h3>
<p>Instructions are provided for completing parts I through VIII of the form, including information on reporting authority, type of issue, description of bonds, uses of proceeds, description of property financed, description of refunded bonds, miscellaneous details, and volume caps.</p>

<h3>Signature and Consent</h3>
<p>An authorized representative of the issuer must sign the form, and if a person other than an officer or employee is authorized to communicate with the IRS, consent for disclosure to that individual is required.</p>

<h3>Paid Preparer</h3>
<p>If a paid preparer is used, they must sign the form, fill in the preparer information, and provide a copy to the issuer.</p>

<p>The instructions also include details on rounding off to whole dollars, late filing procedures, the importance of attaching state certifications for volume cap compliance, and other specific filing details.</p>

<p>For the latest information about developments related to Form 8038 and its instructions, such as legislation enacted after they were published, visit <a href="https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-8038" target="_blank">IRS.gov/Form8038</a>.</p>
