<h2>Summary of Instructions for Form 965-B (Rev. January 2021)</h2>
<p>The Form 965-B is used by corporate taxpayers and Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) to report their net 965 tax liability, including any elections related to section 965 of the Internal Revenue Code. The form also covers reporting for electing REITs regarding section 965 amounts over an 8-year period.</p>

<h3>Future Developments</h3>
<p>For the latest updates on Form 965-B, users are directed to the IRS website.</p>

<h3>What's New</h3>
<p>Changes have been made to the carryback of net operating losses (NOLs) due to the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES Act), affecting reporting on Form 965-B.</p>

<h3>Background</h3>
<p>Section 965 was amended on December 22, 2017, impacting certain taxpayers with foreign income inclusions and deductions.</p>

<h3>General Instructions</h3>
<p>Form 965-B is used to report a taxpayer's net 965 tax liability, including any elections to pay in installments or take into account over an 8-year period for REITs.</p>

<h3>Definitions</h3>
<ul>
  <li><b>Deferred foreign income corporation (DFIC)</b>: A foreign corporation with post-1986 deferred foreign income greater than zero.</li>
  <li><b>U.S. shareholder</b>: A U.S. person with at least 10% voting power in a foreign corporation.</li>
  <li><b>Net income tax liability</b>: The taxpayer's regular income tax liability reduced by certain credits.</li>
  <li><b>Net 965 tax liability</b>: The excess of the taxpayer's net income tax liability with section 965 amounts over the liability without those amounts.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Who Must File</h3>
<p>Corporate taxpayers with a net 965 tax liability or electing REITs with section 965 amounts must file Form 965-B.</p>

<h3>When and Where To File</h3>
<p>Form 965-B should be filed with the taxpayer's income tax return by the due date, including extensions.</p>

<h3>Electronic Filing</h3>
<p>If filing an income tax return electronically, Form 965-B should be included with the electronic submission.</p>

<h3>Specific Instructions</h3>
<p>Instructions are provided for completing the form, including how to report net 965 tax liability, payments, adjustments, and transfers.</p>

<h3>Part I - Report of Net 965 Tax Liability and Election To Pay in Installments</h3>
<p>Part I is used to report the net 965 tax liability and whether the taxpayer elects to pay in installments.</p>

<h3>Part II - Record of Amount of Net 965 Tax Liability Paid by the Taxpayer</h3>
<p>Part II records the actual payments made for the net 965 tax liability reported in Part I.</p>

<h3>Part III - Electing REIT Report of Section 965 Amounts Accounted for Over Time</h3>
<p>Part III is used by electing REITs to report section 965 amounts taken into account over an 8-year period.</p>

<h3>Paperwork Reduction Act Notice</h3>
<p>The form is subject to the Paperwork Reduction Act and is used to ensure compliance with U.S. tax laws.</p>

<p>For detailed instructions and more information, taxpayers are encouraged to refer to the official IRS instructions for Form 965-B.</p>
